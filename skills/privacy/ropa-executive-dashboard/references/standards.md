# Standards and Regulatory References — RoPA Executive Dashboard

## Primary Legislation

### GDPR Articles Supporting RoPA Reporting

- **Art. 38(3)**: The DPO shall directly report to the highest management level of the controller or the processor. RoPA dashboard metrics form a key component of this reporting obligation.
- **Art. 39(1)(b)**: The DPO shall monitor compliance with the GDPR, including the assignment of responsibilities, awareness-raising and training of staff involved in processing operations, and the related audits. Dashboard metrics support this monitoring function.
- **Art. 5(2)**: Accountability — the controller must be able to demonstrate compliance. Dashboard visualisations provide evidence of systematic compliance monitoring.
- **Art. 24(1)**: Controllers must implement measures to ensure and demonstrate processing compliance. Regular dashboard reporting demonstrates ongoing governance.

## Regulatory Guidance

### EDPB

- **EDPB Guidelines on DPO (WP243 rev.01, adopted 13 December 2016, last revised 5 April 2017)**: Recommends that the DPO report regularly to senior management on the state of data protection compliance, including the status of processing records.

### National Supervisory Authorities

- **ICO Accountability Framework**: The ICO's accountability framework tool includes indicators for governance reporting. Organisations scoring "Exceeding" demonstrate regular reporting to the board on data protection metrics including RoPA status.
- **CNIL Annual Activity Report**: CNIL recommends that organisations include RoPA completeness metrics in their annual privacy governance reporting, noting that organisations with dashboard-based monitoring respond more quickly to SA requests.

## Corporate Governance Standards

- **UK Corporate Governance Code (2024)**: Principle O requires the board to establish procedures to manage risk, monitor internal controls, and determine the nature and extent of the principal risks the company is willing to take. Data protection compliance metrics, including RoPA status, feed into this requirement.
- **German Corporate Governance Code (DCGK 2022)**: Recommendation A.3 requires the Management Board to ensure compliance with legal provisions. RoPA dashboard reporting demonstrates compliance monitoring for GDPR obligations.
- **EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)**: While primarily focused on sustainability, the CSRD framework's emphasis on governance metrics creates a parallel expectation for data protection governance reporting.

## ISO/IEC Standards

- **ISO/IEC 27701:2019**: Section 5.4.1 (Management review) requires top management to review the PIMS at planned intervals, including trends in non-conformities and corrective actions. RoPA dashboard metrics directly support this review.
- **ISO 27001:2022**: Clause 9.3 (Management review) requires review of ISMS performance metrics. RoPA compliance scores and trend analysis contribute to this review.
