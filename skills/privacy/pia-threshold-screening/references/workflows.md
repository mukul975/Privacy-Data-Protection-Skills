# Workflow -- PIA Threshold Screening

## Phase 1: Processing Activity Identification

### Step 1.1: Capture Processing Description
- Record the processing activity name, purpose, and scope
- Identify data categories and data subject categories
- Note the technology or systems involved
- Determine the controller establishment (for national blacklist lookup)

### Step 1.2: Classify Processing Context
- New processing activity vs. material change to existing activity
- Internal processing vs. joint controllership vs. processor-based
- Cross-border processing indicator
- Innovation or new technology flag

## Phase 2: Mandatory Trigger Assessment

### Step 2.1: Evaluate Art. 35(3)(a) -- Profiling with Effects
- Does the processing involve systematic and extensive evaluation of personal aspects?
- Is it based on automated processing, including profiling?
- Does it produce legal effects or similarly significant effects on individuals?
- If ALL three conditions are met: **DPIA MANDATORY**

### Step 2.2: Evaluate Art. 35(3)(b) -- Special Category at Scale
- Does the processing involve special category data (Art. 9(1)) or criminal conviction data (Art. 10)?
- Is the processing conducted on a large scale?
- If BOTH conditions are met: **DPIA MANDATORY**

### Step 2.3: Evaluate Art. 35(3)(c) -- Public Area Monitoring
- Does the processing involve systematic monitoring?
- Is it of a publicly accessible area?
- Is it conducted on a large scale?
- If ALL three conditions are met: **DPIA MANDATORY**

## Phase 3: National Blacklist Check

### Step 3.1: Identify Applicable Supervisory Authority
- Determine the lead supervisory authority based on main establishment
- Identify any additional SAs where processing occurs

### Step 3.2: Consult Published DPIA Lists
- Check the Art. 35(4) mandatory DPIA list for the relevant SA
- Check the Art. 35(5) exemption list if available
- Document the specific blacklist entry matched or confirmed as not applicable

## Phase 4: Nine-Criteria Assessment (WP248rev.01)

### Step 4.1: Score Each Criterion
For each of the nine EDPB criteria, assess and record:
- **Met**: The criterion clearly applies to this processing
- **Not Met**: The criterion does not apply
- **Borderline**: Requires further analysis -- document reasoning

### Step 4.2: Apply Decision Threshold
- 2+ criteria met: DPIA required
- 1 criterion met: DPIA recommended; document risk-based decision
- 0 criteria met: DPIA not required; document exemption rationale

## Phase 5: Documentation and Routing

### Step 5.1: Record Screening Decision
- Complete the threshold screening form
- Attach supporting evidence and reasoning
- Obtain DPO review and sign-off

### Step 5.2: Route Outcome
- **DPIA Required**: Create DPIA project in the DPIA register, assign assessor
- **DPIA Recommended**: Escalate to DPO for risk-based decision
- **DPIA Not Required**: Document exemption, link to RoPA entry, schedule periodic re-screening

### Step 5.3: Periodic Re-screening
- Schedule re-screening at defined intervals (annually or upon material change)
- Flag RoPA entries lacking a current screening decision
- Trigger re-screening when regulatory guidance changes (new blacklist items)
