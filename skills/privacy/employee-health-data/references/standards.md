# Standards and Regulatory References — Employee Health Data

## Primary Legislation

### GDPR
- Art. 4(15): Definition of data concerning health.
- Art. 9(1): General prohibition on health data processing.
- Art. 9(2)(a): Explicit consent — rarely valid in employment.
- Art. 9(2)(b): Employment, social security, and social protection obligations.
- Art. 9(2)(c): Vital interests — emergency medical situations.
- Art. 9(2)(h): Preventive or occupational medicine, assessment of working capacity.
- Art. 9(2)(i): Public health — pandemic response.
- Art. 9(3): Processing by or under responsibility of health professional under secrecy.

### EU Directives
- Framework Directive 89/391/EEC: Employer obligations for occupational health surveillance.
- Working Time Directive 2003/88/EC: Health and safety requirements.
- Employment Equality Directive 2000/78/EC: Disability and reasonable adjustments.

### National Law
- **UK**: DPA 2018 Schedule 1 Part 1 Para 1; Equality Act 2010; Access to Medical Reports Act 1988.
- **Germany**: BDSG Section 26(3); ArbSchG (Occupational Safety Act); ArbMedVV (Occupational Health Surveillance Ordinance).
- **France**: Labour Code Art. L.4624-1 et seq.; employer receives fit/unfit only.
- **Netherlands**: UAVG Art. 30(1)(a); Wet verbetering poortwachter (Gatekeeper Improvement Act).
- **Italy**: D.Lgs. 81/2008 Art. 25, 41; medico competente framework.
