## Art. 49(1)(c) — Contract in Interest of Data Subject Assessment

| Condition | Met? | Evidence |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Contract exists between controller and third party | Yes | Insurance policy contract between Athena Global Logistics GmbH and Meridian Cargo Insurance Ltd |
| Contract concluded in interest of the data subject | Yes | Customer requested cargo insurance for their shipment; policy protects the customer's financial interest in the cargo |
| Transfer is objectively necessary for the contract | Yes | Insurer requires consignor details and cargo information to underwrite and issue the policy |
| Transfer is occasional | Yes | One-time transfer for a single insurance policy |
| Data minimisation applied | Yes | Only data required for policy issuance transferred; no financial or identity data beyond policy requirements |

## Necessity Analysis

The transfer of the customer's name, shipping address, cargo description, and declared value to Meridian Cargo Insurance Ltd is objectively necessary for the insurer to assess the risk, underwrite the policy, and issue the insurance certificate. Without these details, the insurance contract cannot be concluded. The customer specifically requested this insurance coverage as part of their freight forwarding arrangement with Athena Global Logistics.

Alternative assessment: Athena considered arranging insurance with EU-based insurers, but the customer specifically requested Meridian Cargo Insurance due to their specialised coverage for fine art shipments to the Caribbean. No EU-based insurer offers equivalent coverage for this route and cargo type.

## Safeguards Applied

Despite the derogation, the following safeguards are applied:

| Safeguard | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Data minimisation | Only data fields required for insurance underwriting are transferred |
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.3 for electronic transmission of policy documents |
| Contractual confidentiality | Insurance contract includes standard confidentiality provisions |
| Retention limitation | Insurer retains data only for the policy duration plus statutory record-keeping period |
| Customer information | Customer informed of the transfer as part of the insurance arrangement disclosure |

## Conclusion

The transfer qualifies under Art. 49(1)(c) as it is necessary for the conclusion of an insurance contract that is in the direct interest of the data subject (protecting their cargo). The transfer is one-time, concerns a single data subject, involves low-sensitivity commercial data, and safeguards have been applied.
