# Standards and Regulatory References

## Primary Legislation

### GDPR Article 56 -- Lead Supervisory Authority
- **Art. 56(1)**: Authority of the main establishment is competent as lead for cross-border processing.
- **Art. 56(2)**: Each authority retains competence for local complaints or local-only infringements.
- **Art. 56(6)**: Lead authority is the sole interlocutor for cross-border matters.

### GDPR Article 4(16) -- Main Establishment
- Controllers: place of central administration in the Union, unless processing decisions are made elsewhere.
- Processors: place of central administration in the Union, or where main processing occurs.

### GDPR Articles 60-66 -- Cooperation
- **Art. 60**: Lead authority cooperates with concerned authorities for consensus.
- **Art. 65**: EDPB dispute resolution when authorities disagree.

## Regulatory Guidance and Enforcement

- **EDPB Guidelines 8/2022 on Identifying Lead Authority (adopted 28 March 2023)**: Criteria for determining main establishment and lead authority.
- **WhatsApp Ireland (DPC, 2021, EUR 225M)**: Landmark cross-border one-stop-shop case with Art. 65 dispute resolution.

## ISO/IEC Standards

- **ISO/IEC 27701:2019**: Privacy Information Management System extending ISO 27001/27002 with privacy-specific controls aligned to GDPR.
- **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**: Information Security Management System providing security foundation for GDPR compliance.
