# Standards and Regulatory References — Whistleblower Data Protection

## Primary Legislation

### EU Whistleblowing Directive 2019/1937
- Art. 6: Conditions for protection — reporting persons must have reasonable grounds.
- Art. 8-9: Internal reporting channels — organisations with 50+ employees.
- Art. 16: Processing of personal data — confidentiality, data minimisation, GDPR compliance.
- Art. 17: Record-keeping — reports stored no longer than necessary and proportionate.
- Art. 18: Retention — comply with confidentiality requirements.
- Art. 19-21: Protection measures — prohibition of retaliation.

### GDPR
- Art. 6(1)(c): Legal obligation — operating the channel required by Directive.
- Art. 6(1)(e): Public interest — investigating reported breaches.
- Art. 9: Special category data — allegations may include sensitive data.
- Art. 10: Criminal offence data — allegations of criminal conduct.
- Art. 14: Right to be informed — accused person's rights, subject to Art. 14(5) restrictions.
- Art. 23: Restrictions — to safeguard investigation integrity.
- Art. 35: DPIA required — vulnerable data subjects, criminal data, significant consequences.

### WP29 Opinion 1/2006 (WP117)
- Whistleblowing limited to serious matters.
- Anonymous reports accepted but not actively encouraged.
- Accused person informed as soon as investigation is not prejudiced.
- Retention: 2 months post-investigation closure.
