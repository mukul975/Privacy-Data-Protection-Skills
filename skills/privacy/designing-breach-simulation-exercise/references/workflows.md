# Breach Simulation Exercise Workflow

## Phase 1: Planning (6-8 Weeks Before Exercise)

1. Define exercise objectives: What capabilities are being tested? (e.g., detection-to-notification chain, cross-functional communication, media response readiness.)
2. Select exercise type: Tabletop (discussion), functional (hands-on), or full-scale (live simulation).
3. Identify participants and assign roles.
4. Develop the scenario narrative with complexity layers and injects.
5. Prepare inject cards (each containing: timestamp, information presented, decision point expected, expected response, evaluation criteria).
6. Schedule the exercise; distribute pre-reading materials to participants (breach response procedure, escalation matrix, notification templates).

## Phase 2: Pre-Exercise Preparation (2 Weeks Before)

1. Facilitator conducts a dry run with the observer/scribe to verify inject timing and flow.
2. Prepare the exercise room: large screen for inject display, printed role cards, clocks showing simulated time, blank flip charts for decision logging.
3. Distribute a participant guide with: exercise objectives, ground rules, role descriptions, and logistics.
4. Confirm all participants' availability and arrange executive backup if a key participant is unavailable.

## Phase 3: Exercise Execution (Exercise Day)

1. **Opening brief (15 min)**: Facilitator explains objectives, ground rules (this is a learning exercise not an evaluation, no phones, maintain simulated time), and scenario background.
2. **Inject delivery**: Facilitator presents injects at scheduled intervals. Each inject includes new information that requires the team to make decisions.
3. **Discussion and decision-making**: Participants discuss each inject within their roles. The Incident Commander coordinates the response. Decisions are recorded by the scribe.
4. **Real-time observation**: Observers note communication patterns, decision quality, procedure adherence, and time management.
5. **Break**: Mid-exercise break (15 minutes for a 3.5-hour exercise).
6. **Final injects and resolution**: Present the final scenario developments and allow the team to bring the exercise to a conclusion.
7. **Hot wash (15 min)**: Immediate debrief while observations are fresh. Each participant shares one thing that went well and one improvement opportunity.

## Phase 4: After-Action Analysis (2 Weeks After Exercise)

1. Facilitator compiles observations from the scribe, observers, and their own notes.
2. Review the decision log against expected responses and evaluate decision quality.
3. Identify gaps in procedure, communication, technical capability, and training.
4. Draft the after-action report with findings, gap analysis, and improvement actions.
5. Present the after-action report to participants and senior management.
6. Enter improvement actions into the remediation tracking system with owners and deadlines.

## Phase 5: Improvement Implementation (Ongoing)

1. Track improvement actions to completion.
2. Update breach response procedures based on lessons learned.
3. Schedule the next exercise (semi-annual for tabletop, annual for functional).
4. Incorporate the exercise and its outcomes in the annual DPO report to the board.
