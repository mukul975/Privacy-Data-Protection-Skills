# Workflow -- DPIA Mitigation Planning

## Phase 1: Mitigation Identification

### Step 1.1: Review Risk Register
- Extract all risks from the DPIA risk scoring phase
- Prioritise by inherent risk level (Very High first)
- Identify risks requiring immediate mitigation vs planned measures

### Step 1.2: Identify Candidate Measures
- For each risk, identify applicable technical measures (encryption, access controls, pseudonymisation, anonymisation, monitoring)
- For each risk, identify applicable organisational measures (policies, training, contracts, audits, governance)
- Consider data protection by design principles (Art. 25) as mitigation measures
- Reference ENISA measure catalogue and ISO 29134 Annex B

### Step 1.3: Assess Measure Feasibility
- Technical feasibility: can the measure be implemented with available technology?
- Financial feasibility: is the cost proportionate to the risk reduction?
- Operational feasibility: does the measure disrupt processing purposes?
- Legal feasibility: are there regulatory constraints on certain measures?

## Phase 2: Mitigation Plan Construction

### Step 2.1: Define Each Measure
- Description: specific control to be implemented
- Type: technical or organisational
- Risk reference: which risk(s) it addresses
- Expected effect: reduces likelihood, reduces severity, or both
- Expected risk reduction: quantified decrease in risk score

### Step 2.2: Assign Ownership and Timeline
- Implementation owner: named individual or team
- Budget allocation: approved funding
- Start date and target completion date
- Dependencies: other measures or projects that must complete first

### Step 2.3: Define Verification Criteria
- How will effectiveness be measured?
- What constitutes successful implementation?
- Testing methodology: penetration test, audit, user acceptance testing
- Ongoing monitoring requirements

## Phase 3: Residual Risk Assessment

### Step 3.1: Calculate Residual Risk
- Re-assess likelihood after applying all planned measures
- Severity typically unchanged (worst-case impact persists)
- Calculate residual risk score
- Map to risk level (Low, Medium, High, Very High)

### Step 3.2: Compare Against Risk Appetite
- Is residual risk within the organisational risk appetite?
- If not, identify additional measures or accept escalation

### Step 3.3: Art. 36 Prior Consultation Assessment
- If any residual risk remains High or Very High, Art. 36 consultation required
- Prepare prior consultation submission package
- Document all measures attempted and why residual risk persists

## Phase 4: Implementation Tracking

### Step 4.1: Track Progress
- Monitor implementation status for each measure
- Update DPIA documentation as measures move through stages
- Flag overdue measures for escalation

### Step 4.2: Verify Effectiveness
- Conduct planned verification activities
- Document verification results
- Update residual risk scores based on verified effectiveness

### Step 4.3: Ongoing Review
- Schedule periodic review of mitigation measure effectiveness
- Trigger re-assessment when threat landscape changes
- Update mitigation plan when new risks emerge or existing risks change
