# Regulatory Standards -- DPIA Mitigation Planning

## Primary Legislation

### GDPR Article 35(7)(d) -- Measures to Address Risks
A DPIA shall contain "the measures envisaged to address the risks, including safeguards, security measures and mechanisms to ensure the protection of personal data and to demonstrate compliance with this Regulation taking into account the rights and legitimate interests of data subjects and other persons concerned."

### GDPR Article 25 -- Data Protection by Design and by Default
Art. 25(1): "The controller shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures designed to implement data-protection principles in an effective manner and to integrate the necessary safeguards into the processing."
Art. 25(2): "The controller shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures for ensuring that, by default, only personal data which are necessary for each specific purpose of the processing are processed."

### GDPR Article 32 -- Security of Processing
Art. 32(1): Controller and processor shall implement measures including:
- (a) pseudonymisation and encryption
- (b) ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability and resilience
- (c) ability to restore availability and access in a timely manner
- (d) process for regularly testing, assessing and evaluating effectiveness

### GDPR Article 36(1) -- Prior Consultation
"The controller shall consult the supervisory authority prior to processing where a data protection impact assessment under Article 35 indicates that the processing would result in a high risk in the absence of measures taken by the controller to mitigate the risk."

### GDPR Recital 84 -- DPIA Purpose
Confirms that the DPIA should include "measures, safeguards and mechanisms envisaged for mitigating that risk, ensuring the protection of personal data and demonstrating compliance."

## Regulatory Guidance

### EDPB WP248rev.01 -- DPIA Guidelines
- Section III.D.c: "Assess necessity and proportionality" including measures under Art. 35(7)(b)
- Section III.D.d: "Manage the risks to the rights and freedoms of data subjects" under Art. 35(7)(c)-(d)
- Clarifies that measures must be "measures envisaged" not just existing controls
- Emphasises proportionality of mitigation to risk level

### ENISA Guidelines on DPIA (2019)
- Provides catalogue of technical and organisational measures mapped to threat scenarios
- Recommends effectiveness assessment methodology for each measure type
- Links measures to specific ENISA threat taxonomy categories

### ISO/IEC 29134:2023 -- Privacy Impact Assessment Guidelines
- Section 7.7: Risk treatment (avoid, reduce, transfer, accept)
- Section 7.8: Residual risk documentation
- Annex B: Example mitigation measures by risk category

### CNIL DPIA Methodology (PIA Tool v3, 2024)
- Maps controls to "existing or planned measures"
- Distinguishes between measures reducing likelihood and measures reducing severity
- Provides maturity assessment for each control

## Enforcement Precedents

### EDPS Formal Opinion on EU Institutions DPIAs
Requires that each identified risk has a documented mitigation measure with implementation status and verified effectiveness.

### ICO Enforcement Notice -- Clearview AI (2022)
Found that the controller failed to implement adequate mitigation measures for biometric data processing risks identified through the DPIA process.
