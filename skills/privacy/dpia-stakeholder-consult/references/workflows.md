# Workflow -- DPIA Stakeholder Consultation

## Phase 1: Consultation Planning

### Step 1.1: Assess Appropriateness
- Review Art. 35(9) -- is consultation appropriate for this processing?
- Consider security implications: would disclosure compromise processing security?
- Consider commercial sensitivity: can processing be described without revealing trade secrets?
- Document decision: if not consulting, record detailed justification

### Step 1.2: Map Stakeholders
- Identify direct data subject groups affected by the processing
- Identify representative bodies (consumer associations, patient groups, trade unions, works councils)
- Confirm DPO involvement per Art. 35(2)
- Identify internal stakeholders (business owners, IT, legal, compliance)
- Determine processor representatives who should participate

### Step 1.3: Design Consultation Strategy
- Select consultation method(s) appropriate to stakeholder groups
- Prepare accessible, plain-language consultation materials
- Define specific questions and areas where input is sought
- Set consultation timeline (minimum 4 weeks recommended for written consultations)
- Allocate budget and resources

## Phase 2: Material Preparation

### Step 2.1: Create Consultation Pack
- Processing activity summary (purpose, data categories, recipients, retention)
- Explanation of identified risks and proposed mitigation measures
- Specific questions for consultees (structured and open-ended)
- Privacy notice for the consultation activity itself
- Response mechanism (online form, email, postal address)

### Step 2.2: Accessibility Review
- Plain language check (no legal jargon without explanation)
- Multi-language provision where data subjects speak different languages
- Accessible format options (large print, screen-reader compatible)
- Reasonable timeframe for responses

## Phase 3: Consultation Execution

### Step 3.1: Distribute and Engage
- Issue consultation materials through agreed channels
- Hold information sessions or focus groups as planned
- Provide dedicated contact point for questions
- Send reminders at midpoint and before deadline

### Step 3.2: Collect Responses
- Log all responses received with timestamps
- Categorise responses by stakeholder group
- Identify emerging themes and specific concerns
- Note response rate for representativeness assessment

## Phase 4: Analysis and Integration

### Step 4.1: Analyse Feedback
- Summarise views received by theme
- Identify concerns that align with identified DPIA risks
- Identify new risks or impacts raised by consultees
- Assess whether proposed mitigations address consultee concerns

### Step 4.2: Integrate into DPIA
- Update DPIA risk assessment where consultee input reveals new risks
- Modify mitigation measures where consultee feedback suggests improvements
- Document where consultee views were adopted and the changes made
- Document where consultee views were not adopted with clear reasoning

## Phase 5: Feedback and Record-Keeping

### Step 5.1: Close the Loop
- Notify consultees of DPIA outcome and actions taken
- Publish consultation summary where appropriate
- Explain how their input influenced the assessment

### Step 5.2: Documentation
- File complete consultation record with DPIA documentation
- Include: materials distributed, responses received, analysis, decisions made
- Retain as evidence for Art. 5(2) accountability and potential SA review
- Link consultation record to DPIA register entry
