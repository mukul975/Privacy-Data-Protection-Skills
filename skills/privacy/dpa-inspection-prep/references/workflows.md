# DPA Inspection Preparation Workflows

## Workflow 1: Unannounced Inspection Response

1. **Reception Alert** (Immediate): Reception notifies DPO/Legal by phone
2. **DPO Arrives** (within 15 minutes): DPO or designated privacy counsel reaches reception
3. **Credential Verification**: Verify inspector ID and authorization letter
4. **Scope Confirmation**: Confirm inspection scope, legal basis, and processing activities covered
5. **Escort Assignment**: Assign escort(s) to accompany inspectors at all times
6. **Note-Taker Assignment**: Designate note-taker for all interactions
7. **Meeting Room Setup**: Provide dedicated meeting room for inspectors
8. **Document Provision**: Provide requested documents (copies only, not originals)
9. **Interview Facilitation**: Make requested personnel available for interviews
10. **Ongoing Communication**: Keep senior management informed of progress
11. **Closing Conference**: Obtain list of follow-up items and deadlines
12. **Internal Debrief** (within 2 hours): Debrief with all involved personnel

## Workflow 2: Written Inquiry Response

1. **Receipt and Logging** (Day 0): DPO receives and logs inquiry in correspondence register
2. **Acknowledgment** (Day 1-2): DPO sends formal acknowledgment to DPA
3. **Legal Review** (Day 2-3): Legal reviews scope and legal basis of inquiry
4. **Assignment** (Day 3-5): DPO assigns response sections to subject matter experts
5. **Drafting** (Day 5-12): Assigned owners draft responses with supporting evidence
6. **DPO Review** (Day 12-14): DPO reviews all responses for accuracy and completeness
7. **Legal Review** (Day 14-16): Legal reviews for privilege, strategy, and risk
8. **Approval** (Day 16-18): Senior management approves (if material)
9. **Submission** (Day 18-20): Submit response via DPA's specified channel
10. **Follow-Up** (Day 20+): Track any additional requests or follow-up

## Workflow 3: Announced Audit Preparation

### 8 Weeks Before Audit
1. Receive audit notification; confirm scope, dates, and logistics
2. Assemble preparation team (DPO, Legal, CISO, business process owners)
3. Review document readiness checklist; begin addressing any Red items
4. Schedule preparation meetings for each audit topic

### 4 Weeks Before Audit
5. Conduct mock interviews with key personnel
6. Prepare technical demonstration environment
7. Compile evidence binders organized by audit topic
8. Conduct internal pre-audit to identify vulnerabilities

### 2 Weeks Before Audit
9. Final document readiness check (target: all Green)
10. Brief all interviewees on principles (factual, precise, no volunteering)
11. Confirm logistics (rooms, equipment, parking, refreshments)
12. Prepare contact sheet for all personnel the DPA may need

### During Audit
13. Opening meeting with audit team
14. Provide documents as requested
15. Facilitate interviews and demonstrations
16. Daily internal debrief

### Post-Audit
17. Closing meeting; obtain preliminary findings
18. Internal debrief within 24 hours
19. Create action register for all commitments
20. Begin response preparation for any findings

## Workflow 4: Document Readiness Maintenance

1. **Monthly Check**: Review core documents (top 15) for currency
2. **Quarterly Check**: Review supporting documents (items 16-30) for currency
3. **Update as Triggered**: Update documents when processing changes occur
4. **Readiness Scoring**: Score document readiness quarterly (target: >90% Green)
5. **Annual Review**: Full document readiness assessment with remediation plan
