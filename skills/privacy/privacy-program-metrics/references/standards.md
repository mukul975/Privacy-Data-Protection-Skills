# Privacy Program Metrics Standards Reference

## Primary Frameworks

### NIST Privacy Framework v1.0 — Govern-P Function
- **GV.MT**: Privacy program monitoring and measurement
- **Application**: Establishes governance expectations for privacy metrics and reporting

### ISO 27001:2022 — Clause 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Evaluation
- **Requirement**: Organization shall determine what needs to be monitored and measured, methods, when, and who shall analyze results
- **Application to Privacy**: Extended via ISO 27701 to include privacy-specific measurements

### AICPA GAPP — Management Principle
- **Criteria 1.2.9**: The entity monitors compliance with its privacy policies and procedures
- **Application**: Establishes requirement for ongoing compliance measurement

## Metric Design Principles

### SMART Metrics
- **Specific**: Clearly defined measurement with unambiguous interpretation
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable with a reliable data source
- **Achievable**: Targets are realistic given organizational context
- **Relevant**: Metric drives meaningful privacy outcomes
- **Time-bound**: Measured at defined intervals with trend tracking

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators (NIST/IIA Framework)
- **Leading**: Predictive measures enabling proactive intervention
- **Lagging**: Retrospective measures confirming effectiveness of past actions
