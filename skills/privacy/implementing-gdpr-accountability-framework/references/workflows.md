# Accountability Framework Workflow Reference

## Framework Establishment Workflow

1. **Board mandate**: Obtain formal board resolution endorsing data protection as a strategic priority and authorising the allocation of resources for the accountability framework.
2. **Gap assessment**: Conduct a baseline assessment of existing accountability measures against the documentation inventory (Tiers 1-4). Identify missing, incomplete, or outdated documents.
3. **Prioritisation**: Rank gaps by regulatory risk and effort. Critical gaps (no RoPA, no DPO, no breach process) are addressed first.
4. **Resource allocation**: Assign budget, headcount, and tools. Typical resources include: DPO office staff, privacy management platform, document management system, e-learning platform.
5. **Milestone plan**: Create a 12-month implementation plan with quarterly milestones and board-level progress reporting.

## Documentation Lifecycle Workflow

For each accountability document:

1. **Draft**: Author creates initial version using the approved template. Content must be specific to the organisation, not generic placeholder text.
2. **DPO review**: DPO reviews for GDPR alignment, accuracy, and completeness.
3. **Stakeholder review**: Relevant business owners review for operational accuracy and feasibility.
4. **Approval**: Appropriate authority approves (board for Tier 1, DPO for Tier 2-4).
5. **Publication**: Document is published in the accountability portfolio, version-controlled, and distributed to relevant staff.
6. **Training**: Staff are trained on new or updated documents where the document affects their operational responsibilities.
7. **Review cycle**: Set the review date:
   - Tier 1 (Governance): Annual review by board
   - Tier 2 (Operational): Annual review by DPO, or upon material change
   - Tier 3 (Incident): Continuous updates; annual completeness review
   - Tier 4 (Assurance): Per audit/training cycle
8. **Retirement**: Obsolete versions are archived with clear marking and retained for a minimum of 6 years to evidence historical compliance.

## Board Reporting Workflow

1. **Quarterly reporting**: DPO presents a dashboard to the Data Protection Steering Committee covering:
   - DSAR volumes and response times
   - Breach count and severity
   - DPIA pipeline status
   - Training completion rates
   - Open audit findings
   - Regulatory developments

2. **Annual reporting**: DPO presents a comprehensive annual report to the Board covering:
   - Overall compliance posture and maturity trend
   - Key risks and mitigations
   - Regulatory enforcement trends relevant to the organisation
   - Budget utilisation and resource needs for the next year
   - Recommendation for strategic investments

3. **Incident reporting**: Material breaches (Art. 33 notifiable) and significant regulatory developments are reported to the Board within 72 hours.

## Supervisory Authority Response Workflow

When the supervisory authority requests accountability evidence under Art. 58(1):

1. **Receipt and triage**: Log the request, assign a response lead (typically DPO), and determine the scope of information requested.
2. **Evidence assembly**: Compile relevant documents from the accountability portfolio. Ensure documents are current and internally consistent.
3. **Legal review**: General Counsel reviews the response for legal privilege considerations and completeness.
4. **Response**: Submit within the timeframe specified by the authority (typically 4 weeks unless urgent). Cooperate fully per Art. 31.
5. **Follow-up**: Track any additional requests or findings from the authority. Update the accountability portfolio based on feedback.

## Continuous Improvement Workflow

1. **Monitor inputs**: Collect data from audits, incidents, DSARs, complaints, regulatory guidance, and industry benchmarks.
2. **Analyse trends**: Quarterly trend analysis of compliance metrics to identify systemic issues.
3. **Update framework**: Revise policies, procedures, and controls based on trend analysis and lessons learned.
4. **Test effectiveness**: Conduct targeted testing (walkthroughs, simulations, sample reviews) to verify improvements.
5. **Report progress**: Include improvement actions and outcomes in the quarterly and annual DPO reports.
