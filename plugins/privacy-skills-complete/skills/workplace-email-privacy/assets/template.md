## 1. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers who use the organisation's electronic communications systems, including:
- Corporate email systems
- Internet access via the corporate network
- Instant messaging and collaboration platforms (Microsoft Teams, Slack)
- Telephone systems
- Personal devices used to access corporate systems (BYOD)

### 3.2 Why Monitoring Takes Place

The organisation monitors electronic communications for the following specific purposes:
- [ ] Preventing data loss and protecting confidential business information
- [ ] Detecting and preventing security threats (malware, phishing)
- [ ] Ensuring compliance with regulatory obligations
- [ ] Enforcing this Acceptable Use Policy
- [ ] Investigating specific, documented suspected misconduct
- [ ] Other: _____________

### 3.3 Who Has Access to Monitoring Data

| Role | Access | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| IT Security Team | Automated DLP alerts; metadata logs | Security threat detection |
| Compliance Officer | Regulatory investigation data | Regulatory compliance |
| HR (authorised cases only) | Escalated cases from IT Security | Disciplinary investigation |
| Line Managers | **No direct access** to monitoring data | N/A |

### 3.4 What Is Excluded from Monitoring

The following categories of communications are excluded from monitoring:
- Communications with legal counsel (legal professional privilege)
- Communications with medical professionals
- Communications with trade union representatives
- Communications marked as personal (where personal use is permitted)

## 4. Consequences

Violation of this policy may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension of access to specific systems
- Disciplinary action up to and including dismissal for serious violations

**Important**: Monitoring data will not be used as the sole basis for any adverse employment decision. Human review is required before any disciplinary action.

## 5. Your Rights

As an employee, you have the following rights regarding monitoring data:
- **Right of access**: You may request a copy of any monitoring data held about you (Data Subject Access Request)
- **Right to challenge**: You may challenge any decision made using monitoring data through the organisation's grievance procedure
- **Right to complain**: You may complain to the Data Protection Officer or the supervisory authority ([ICO / CNIL / BfDI / etc.])
