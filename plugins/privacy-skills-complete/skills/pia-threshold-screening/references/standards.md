# Regulatory Standards -- PIA Threshold Screening

## Primary Legislation

### GDPR Article 35(1) -- DPIA Obligation
"Where a type of processing in particular using new technologies, and taking into account the nature, scope, context and purposes of the processing, is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the controller shall, prior to the processing, carry out an assessment of the impact of the envisaged processing operations on the protection of personal data."

### GDPR Article 35(3) -- Mandatory DPIA Cases
Specifies three categories where a DPIA is always required:
- (a) Systematic and extensive profiling-based evaluation with legal/significant effects
- (b) Large-scale special category or criminal conviction data processing
- (c) Large-scale systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas

### GDPR Article 35(4) -- Supervisory Authority Lists
"The supervisory authority shall establish and make public a list of the kind of processing operations which are subject to the requirement for a data protection impact assessment."

### GDPR Article 35(5) -- Exemption Lists
Supervisory authorities may also establish lists of processing not requiring a DPIA.

### GDPR Article 35(10) -- Exemption for Legal Basis
DPIA not required where processing has a legal basis in EU or Member State law which the controller is subject to, and that law regulates the specific processing operation, and a DPIA was already carried out as part of a general impact assessment in the context of adopting that legal basis.

## Regulatory Guidance

### EDPB (formerly Article 29 Working Party) Guidelines on DPIAs (WP248rev.01)
- Adopted 4 April 2017, last revised 4 October 2017
- Establishes nine criteria for identifying high-risk processing
- Recommends DPIA when two or more criteria are met
- Provides examples and worked screening scenarios
- Clarifies "large scale" and "systematic monitoring" interpretation

### EDPB Opinion 6/2019 on DPIA Lists
Provides consistency opinions on the national supervisory authority DPIA blacklists, ensuring harmonised application across the EEA.

## National Supervisory Authority Blacklists

### Germany -- BfDI / State DPAs
Konferenz der unabhangigen Datenschutzaufsichtsbehorden published a common DPIA blacklist (Muss-Liste) covering 17 processing categories including:
- Large-scale profiling
- AI-based decision systems
- Biometric identification in public spaces
- Tracking of location data

### France -- CNIL
Published DPIA required list (14 categories) and not-required list per Art. 35(4)-(5), updated periodically.

### Ireland -- DPC
Section 84 Data Protection Act 2018 list specifying processing requiring DPIA in the Irish context.

### Netherlands -- Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens
Published list under Besluit DPIA covering processing with heightened risk characteristics.

## Enforcement Precedents

### AEPD (Spain) -- PS/00547/2021
Fine of EUR 2.5 million against CaixaBank for failure to conduct a DPIA before implementing a profiling system affecting 13 million customers. The screening process failed to identify the mandatory Art. 35(3)(a) trigger.

### ICO (UK) -- Clearview AI (2022)
Fine of GBP 7.5 million. ICO found that Clearview failed to conduct a DPIA for its facial recognition service constituting large-scale biometric processing and systematic monitoring. No threshold screening was documented.

### CNIL (France) -- Criteo SA (2023)
Fine of EUR 40 million. Among the violations, CNIL found inadequate DPIA process for large-scale profiling across millions of users. The controller had no documented screening mechanism.
