# Regulatory Standards — Colorado Privacy Act

## Primary Legislation

### Colorado Privacy Act — C.R.S. §6-1-1301 through §6-1-1313

Enacted: July 7, 2021 (SB 21-190). Effective: July 1, 2023.

- **§6-1-1301**: Short title
- **§6-1-1302**: Legislative declaration
- **§6-1-1303**: Definitions
- **§6-1-1304**: Applicability and exemptions
- **§6-1-1305**: Right of consumers to opt out of processing (sale, targeted advertising, profiling)
- **§6-1-1306**: Consumer rights (access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out)
- **§6-1-1307**: Consumer rights request processing and appeal
- **§6-1-1308**: Controller duties
- **§6-1-1309**: Data protection assessments
- **§6-1-1310**: Processor duties
- **§6-1-1311**: Enforcement by AG
- **§6-1-1312**: Cure period (60 days until January 1, 2025; discretionary thereafter)
- **§6-1-1313**: Relation to other laws

## AG Implementing Regulations — 4 CCR 904-3

### Final Rules (Effective March 1, 2024)

**Rule 2 — Definitions:**
- Clarifies "bona fide loyalty program" exemption parameters
- Defines "universal opt-out mechanism" technical requirements
- Specifies "profiling" scope for opt-out purposes

**Rule 3 — Consumer Rights:**
- §3.01: Request submission methods (at least one accessible to consumers with disabilities)
- §3.02: Authentication requirements (commercially reasonable, proportionate to risk)
- §3.03: Response requirements (45 days, extensible by 45)
- §3.04: Appeal procedures (written appeal, 45-day response, AG referral option)
- §3.05: Free response requirement (at least once per 12 months per right)

**Rule 4 — Controller and Processor:**
- §4.01: Privacy notice content requirements
- §4.02: Data minimization standards
- §4.03: Purpose limitation enforcement
- §4.04: Processor contract requirements
- §4.05: De-identified data standards (reasonable technical safeguards, prohibition on re-identification, public commitment)

**Rule 5 — Universal Opt-Out:**
- §5.01: Technical specifications for opt-out mechanisms
- §5.10: Controller recognition requirements (effective July 1, 2024)
- §5.11: Signal processing requirements (no pop-ups, no additional verification)
- §5.12: Authenticated vs. unauthenticated consumer handling
- §5.13: Conflict resolution (most recent preference prevails)
- §5.14: AG biennial review of recognized mechanisms

**Rule 6 — Sensitive Data Consent:**
- §6.01: Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- §6.02: Separate consent per sensitive data category and purpose
- §6.03: Consent withdrawal mechanism (must be as easy as granting)
- §6.04: Children's data — opt-in consent from parent or guardian for known children

**Rule 7 — Data Protection Assessments:**
- §7.01: Triggers for DPIA
- §7.02: Content requirements (proportionality analysis, de-identification consideration)
- §7.03: AG access (must be made available upon request)
- §7.04: Retention period (at least 3 years from completion of processing activity)

## Colorado Consumer Protection Act — C.R.S. §6-1-101 et seq.

CPA violations are treated as deceptive trade practices under the broader Colorado Consumer Protection Act, providing the AG with established enforcement mechanisms including:
- Civil penalties up to $20,000 per violation (§6-1-112)
- Injunctive relief (§6-1-110)
- Restitution for affected consumers (§6-1-113)
- Costs and attorney fees

## Comparison: Colorado Universal Opt-Out vs. California GPC

| Aspect | Colorado (4 CCR 904-3 Rule 5) | California (CPPA Regs §7025) |
|--------|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Effective date | July 1, 2024 | Effective with CPRA (Jan 1, 2023) |
| Signal scope | Targeted advertising + sale | Sale + sharing |
| Profiling opt-out via signal | Not automatically — only targeted ads and sale | Not automatically — only sale and sharing |
| Affirmative selection required | Yes — cannot be default | Not specified (GPC may be default in browser) |
| Pop-up prohibition | Yes | Yes |
| Verification prohibition | Yes | Yes |
| Conflict resolution | Most recent preference | GPC takes precedence as most recent expression |
