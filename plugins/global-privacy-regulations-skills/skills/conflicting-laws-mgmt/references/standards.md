# Standards and Regulatory References — Conflicting Laws Management

## International Frameworks for Conflict Resolution

### OECD Privacy Guidelines (2013)
- Principle of interoperability: privacy frameworks should be developed to work with other frameworks
- Encouraged consistent implementation to reduce cross-border conflicts
- Accountability principle enables flexible approaches to compliance

### APEC Privacy Framework (2015 update)
- Cross-Border Privacy Rules (CBPR) designed to reduce conflicts between APEC economies
- Provides a common baseline that member economies recognise
- Does not eliminate conflicts but reduces them for participating organisations

### Council of Europe Convention 108+ (2018)
- Modernised international standard for data protection
- Designed to be compatible with national laws
- Art. 14: Transborder flows with safeguards to prevent circumvention

## Resolution Principles

### Principle 1: Highest Common Denominator
Apply the most protective standard where requirements are compatible but differ in stringency.

### Principle 2: Jurisdictional Specificity
Where requirements are truly incompatible, implement jurisdiction-specific controls and document the conflict resolution.

### Principle 3: Risk-Based Approach
Where no resolution fully satisfies all jurisdictions, adopt the approach that minimises aggregate risk, considering enforcement likelihood, penalty exposure, and data subject impact.

### Principle 4: Transparency
Document all conflicts and resolutions transparently for regulatory inquiry.
