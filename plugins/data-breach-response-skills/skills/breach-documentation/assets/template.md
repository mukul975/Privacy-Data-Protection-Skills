## Executive Summary

During 2025, Stellar Payments Group recorded 14 personal data breaches in the Art. 33(5) breach register. Of these, 5 required supervisory authority notification under Art. 33(1) and 2 required data subject notification under Art. 34(1). The total number of affected data subjects across all breaches was 18,947. All remediation actions for closed breaches have been completed. Two breaches from Q4 2025 have ongoing remediation with target completion in Q1 2026.

## Recommendations

1. **Address human error root cause**: 35.7% of breaches resulted from misdirected communications. Recommend deploying email DLP recipient verification prompts and expanding secure file transfer platform usage.
2. **Strengthen external attack defenses**: Deploy phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2/WebAuthn) for all accounts accessing personal data systems (currently deployed only for privileged accounts).
3. **Improve insider threat detection**: Expand UEBA coverage to include the analytics data warehouse and mobile app backend (gaps identified in the Q4 2025 detection assessment).
4. **Register improvement**: Add a field for "time to detect" (breach timestamp to discovery timestamp) to enable dwell time trend analysis.
5. **Board reporting cadence**: Continue quarterly breach summary to the Audit Committee with full annual report.
